Bigger Than Us ▷▶


Being blind to color and culture is not the solution, talking about it and acknowledging it is. Team Bigger Than Us aims to bring awareness to how people perceive each other and was made in a response towards current events. Yeab Kebede and Sabrina Kwong conducted interviews after sending out a survey and collecting data. Hence this project serves as a qualitative research-driven video art piece compiled through surveys on the theme of cultural awareness and fighting against racism. Kebede was in charge of in creating illustrations of those interviewed and Kwong was in control of directing the video production. Join us, towards conversations bigger than us.



Medium: Video, Graphic Design

Ye'ab Kebede

Born and raised in a country which embraces the dynamic of colors, patterns and magical folk stories, I am subconsciously attracted to colorful textiles and patterns. As a young child walking through the streets of Ethiopia, I remember that the colors not only had a life of their own but had a distinct smell. As a multi-medium artist I am inspired by my early on relationship with colors and patterns in addition to life itself. I use a wide array of means from traditional painting and drawing to more moderns was like illustration, video, photography and digital media art.

Sabrina Kwong

Sabrina Kwong is a Chinese Born-American artist who creates works of art that center around the concepts of self-identity, cultural upbringing, wellness, and the spaces between bodies through technological and traditional mediums.

In particular, she writes stories of fiction and non-fiction, poetry, paints abstract paintings with watercolors, gouaches, acrylic. Make short films, illustrates with ink and watercolor, and develop conceptual pieces with technology, to process the world poetically.

Currently creating art targeted towards social issues and cultural awareness, their work center towards viewers to be mindful of the power of one's voice and contributions to the greater society.

Kwong also works with pieces that delve into the human experience, empathy, and experimentation. They are a firm believer in sunlight and joy to combat this strange world that we all live in.
